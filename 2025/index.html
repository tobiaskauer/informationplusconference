Information+ Conference 2025
Boston, MA

Information+ is a biennial conference that brings together researchers, educators, and practitioners in data visualization, information design and all design practices. We aim to provoke discussions on how information representation engages within social, political and environmental contexts.

Call for Contributions

The Information+ conference invites submissions for Presentations, Workshops and Activities, and Exhibitions, encouraging interdisciplinary contributions that explore the theme of understanding, designing, and communicating complex information.

Information+ is a biennial conference that brings together researchers and practitioners in information design and data visualization to explore shared questions and address challenges in these rapidly changing fields. Unlike discipline-specific gatherings, Information+ is committed to fostering rich, interdisciplinary exchanges and provoke discussions among those engaged in the theories, practices, and teaching of analyzing and communicating information.

Building on the previous editions, Information+ 2025 will be held as an in-person event in Cambridge, MA, hosted by Northeastern University’s College of Arts, Media, and Design (CAMD) and MIT’s School of Architecture and Planning through the Leventhal Center for Advanced Urbanism (LCAU) and Morningside Academy of Design (MAD). CAMD, LCAU, and MAD actively promote interdisciplinary approaches to teaching and research in information design, data visualization, and related fields.

In past years, Information+ presentations have covered a wide spectrum of topics in information design and visualization. This year, we invite submissions that address a variety of themes, with a focus on both general and domain-specific applications:

General Topics in Information Design and Visualization:

  • Ethical considerations
  • Design processes
  • Education
  • Justice and equity
  • Environmental sustainability
  • Accessibility and disability justice
  • Modalities of data representation (e.g., touch, sound, taste)
  • Interdisciplinary practices
  • Empathy and joy

Domain-Specific Applications of Design and Visualization:

  • Collective futures
  • Indigenous knowledge systems
  • Social and political issues
  • Environment and climate action
  • Health and wellbeing
  • Cultural collections and museums
  • Digital humanities
  • Data journalism
  • Emerging technology
  • Advances and trends in information design

Information+ invites submissions for three types of contributions:

1. Presentations We seek presentations that engage the community in timely, thought-provoking, and interdisciplinary discussions. Contributions are welcomed from practitioners, artists, academics, and others who may not typically be represented at visualization and data-focused conferences. Presentation formats include 5, 15, or 20-minute talks.

Submissions should not exceed 3,000 characters and must be submitted through EasyChair by May 31st. Accepted presentations will be invited for publication in a journal. For more details, visit informationplusconference.com/2023/#cfp.

2. Workshops & Activities Workshops and Activities provide opportunities for discussion, hands-on experiences, and networking, with sessions varying in length and purpose. Activities offer brief exercises to engage conference attendees with data and visual representation.

Submissions should be completed via a provided form and submitted through EasyChair by June 7th. For more details, visit informationplusconference.com/2023/#workshops.

3. Exhibition Entries We welcome exhibition entries that showcase recent innovations in the visual and material communication of information. Submissions are due by June 30th. More information is available at informationplusconference.com/2023/#exhibition.
